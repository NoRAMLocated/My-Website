Week 4 · January 29, 2026 Reflections — Noah Loar Emotional Leadership

The LeaderWithin

From my experience in the first sprint, I found that my leadership style is the coaching leader style. When a team member was stuck — on the slideshow, the interactive element, or interview prep — I did my best to help them get past the roadblock.

"I tried to adopt a different leadership style, but being bossy or leading others in a different direction is not easy for me."

Even though our team was functional and we each completed our specific parts, I feel like I played a role at every step of the process — even when it wasn't my job.

I originally thought I was a strong leader who could change people's minds on solid ideas, but as it turns out, I am not very strong in that area.

Emotional leadership is most effective in situations that evoke strong or extreme emotions — highly situation-driven, especially under high stakes and a frantic team environment.

Leadership Styles
🌱
Coaching

⚡
Commanding

🤝
Affiliative
Builds harmony and emotional bonds within the team.
🔭
Visionary
Mobilizes people toward a shared long-term goal.

What I Learned

Coaching comes naturally — supporting others through difficulty is instinctive. But pitching ideas and redirecting a team toward a new direction requires a level of command I haven't yet developed. Recognizing the gap is the first step toward filling it.

The Tension

I tried to get us to adopt a more technological, interactive element in our project, but had trouble pitching it. The tension between what I saw as a great idea and my ability to advocate for it revealed a real blind spot — influence requires more than a good idea.
