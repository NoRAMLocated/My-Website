01
How the Sprint Unfolded
Day 1

The Leadership Void

We started the sprint without a clear leader. All three of us shared the coaching style — nobody moved to take command. We kept asking each other who wanted the role.

1.5 Periods

Time Lost

Roughly one and a half class periods passed before we settled on roles. All three of us lacked the commanding trait — and it showed in our inability to make a fast decision.

Turning Point

Organizational Traits to the Rescue

We stopped trying to find a "natural leader" and instead used our organizational traits to match each person to the role they were best suited for.

Product Lead

Noah Takes Product Lead

Knowing I had difficulty with time management and presenting, I chose the product lead role. This led to a well-thought-out product — organized enough to bring in Juniors from outside the room.

Takeaway

The Lesson Learned

Each leadership style has its time and place. When groups lack a mix of styles, they tend to be less productive and efficient. A team of all coaches needs at least one commander.

04
Full Analysis

From my experience during this sprint, I found that instead of someone taking a leadership role willingly, we would all ask whether someone else wanted the role.

"Having similar leadership traits was not helpful — we were all underskilled in the commanding trait."

Having a group of three people who all shared the coaching leadership style seemed to be our biggest problem. Eventually, we used our organizational traits to determine who would best fit each role.

Knowing I had more difficulty with time management and presenting, I decided to take on the product lead role — which ultimately led to a well-thought-out product.

The final product was well organized enough to bring in some Juniors who were outside the room.

I have found that each leadership style has its time and place, but when groups lack a mix of styles, they tend to be less productive or efficient.
